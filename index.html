A History of Artificial Intelligence

A visual timeline tracing major AI milestones, breakthroughs, and winters from 1950 through the frontier model era of 2023–2024 — from Alan Turing's imitation game to Claude, GPT-4, and the EU AI Act.

AI History Research Synthesis Timeline Academic

Objective

To synthesize 70+ years of AI evolution into a structured, accurate, and accessible visual timeline — identifying the foundational milestones, cyclical "AI Winters," and the transformative breakthroughs of the deep learning era that continue to shape technology today.

Process

  • Researched primary and secondary sources covering AI history from 1950 to 2024.
  • Identified key events: Turing Test (1950), Dartmouth Conference (1956), both AI Winters, Deep Blue (1997), AlexNet (2012), Transformer architecture (2017), AlphaFold 2 (2021), and ChatGPT (2022).
  • Organized milestones chronologically with legend categories: Foundational, AI Winter, Deep Learning Era, Major Models.
  • Crafted concise, accurate descriptions for each milestone grounded in cited academic sources.

Key Milestones

  • 1950 — Turing Test proposed by Alan Turing
  • 1956 — Dartmouth Conference: AI formally named
  • 1974–1993 — Two AI Winters: funding cuts & expert system collapse
  • 1997 — Deep Blue defeats Kasparov (IBM)
  • 2012 — AlexNet launches the deep learning era
  • 2017 — "Attention Is All You Need" — the Transformer
  • 2021 — AlphaFold 2 solves protein folding (Nobel Prize 2024)
  • 2022 — ChatGPT: 100M users in 2 months
  • 2023–24 — GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, o1 — frontier model race

Key Findings

AI progress is cyclical — periods of inflated expectation followed by funding collapses ("Winters") have recurred throughout the field's history. The modern era is defined by the 2012 deep learning breakthrough and the 2017 Transformer architecture, which underpins virtually every major language model today including BERT, GPT, LLaMA, Claude, and Gemini.

✦ Value Proposition

This artifact demonstrates the ability to research, synthesize, and communicate a complex, multi-decade technical history in a structured and accessible format. It reflects a deep strategic understanding of how AI has evolved — equipping stakeholders with the historical context needed to make informed decisions about AI adoption, strategy, and ethics. Relevant for educators, students, AI practitioners, and any professional navigating the modern AI landscape.

ShopBot: Generative AI Customer Service Assistant

A fully functional AI-powered retail chatbot built using Design Thinking methodology — from empathy mapping and problem definition through prototyping, prompt engineering, and iterative testing on Botpress.

Generative AI Prompt Engineering Design Thinking Chatbot LLM

Objective

To design and build an empathy-driven AI customer service chatbot for an online retail store — capable of handling order inquiries, return policy questions, and product support at scale while maintaining a consistent, brand-aligned tone without human escalation.

Process

  • Empathy: Identified user pain points — long wait times, repetitive explanations, feeling unheard.
  • Define: Problem statement: customers need fast, friendly, accurate support without waiting for a human agent.
  • Ideate: Evaluated rule-based FAQ bot, generative AI assistant, and hybrid human-escalation models.
  • Prototype: Built ShopBot in Botpress using a detailed system prompt with persona, constraints, and return policy specifics.
  • Test: Ran 5 scenarios validating each system prompt rule; made 2 iterative improvements.

LLM Testing (Section 1)

  • Used ChatGPT with a Context / Task / Constraints prompt framework across 3 separate sessions.
  • Chat 1: Warm, empathetic, stayed within word limit — met all criteria.
  • Chat 2: More formal; added unprompted free-shipping offer — showed LLM creativity drift.
  • Chat 3: Most concise and natural-sounding overall — best tone match.
  • Key insight: Same prompt produces meaningfully different outputs — tone, formality, and extras varied.

STORM AI Research (Section 3)

  • Researched "Customer Service Chatbots in E-Commerce" using STORM AI.
  • STORM breaks topics into specific questions (simulating a research interview), then synthesizes structured articles with citations.
  • Output covered chatbot history, current e-commerce applications, challenges (complex queries, human-like tone), and generative AI future trends.
  • Key takeaway: Brainstorming transparency helps evaluate AI-generated information more critically.

Iterations & Challenges

  • Challenge: Bot gave vague answers about return policy — no specifics in prompt.
    Fix: Added explicit policy details (30-day window, original packaging required).
  • Challenge: Opening greeting varied too much between sessions.
    Fix: Added a specific example greeting to anchor tone from the first message.
  • Challenge: Bot answered unrelated general knowledge questions.
    Fix: Added constraint "do not engage in conversations unrelated to the store."
✦ Value Proposition

This artifact demonstrates the ability to bridge the gap between raw LLM technology and a practical, production-ready customer service application. The end-to-end Design Thinking process — from user empathy through iterative prompt engineering — mirrors real-world AI product development. It proves that AI output quality is directly proportional to the quality of instructions given, and that empathy-driven design produces more user-focused, reliable AI systems. Applicable to any industry deploying conversational AI for customer engagement.
