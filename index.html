Satellite Lifespan Prediction Using Machine Learning
Predictive Modeling Regression Feature Engineering Aerospace Data

Introduction

This project applies supervised machine learning techniques to predict the operational lifespan of satellites based on design specifications, orbital parameters, and mission attributes — providing data-driven insights into satellite sustainability and mission planning.

Objective

To build and evaluate regression models capable of predicting how long a satellite will remain operational. The goal is to enable aerospace engineers and mission planners to make better-informed decisions about satellite design, launch timing, and replacement scheduling.

Process

  • Collected and cleaned satellite metadata from publicly available orbital databases, handling missing values and outliers.
  • Engineered features including orbit type, launch mass, power capacity, and expected design life.
  • Trained and compared multiple regression algorithms including Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, and Linear Regression.
  • Evaluated models using RMSE, MAE, and R² metrics; applied cross-validation for generalizability.
  • Visualized feature importances and residual distributions to interpret model behavior.

Key Findings

  • Gradient Boosting achieved the lowest RMSE, outperforming baseline linear models.
  • Orbit type (GEO vs LEO) and design life specification were the strongest predictors of actual lifespan.
  • Power capacity and launch mass showed moderate positive correlation with operational longevity.
  • Model results offer practical utility for risk assessment in satellite insurance and scheduling models.
✦ Value Proposition

Satellite lifespan prediction bridges aerospace engineering and data analytics — providing mission planners, insurers, and space agencies with a quantitative tool to reduce uncertainty. The project demonstrates proficiency in the full ML lifecycle: data preparation, feature engineering, model evaluation, and interpretation. Its relevance extends to any domain requiring predictive maintenance and asset lifecycle management.

02
Instacart Market Basket Analysis & Customer Segmentation
EDA Market Basket Analysis Customer Segmentation Data Visualization

Introduction

This analysis explores the Instacart Online Grocery Shopping dataset to uncover purchasing patterns, identify frequently co-purchased product combinations, and segment customers based on ordering behavior — providing actionable insights for retail personalization and inventory strategy.

Objective

To perform comprehensive exploratory data analysis and apply market basket analysis techniques to understand how customers shop on Instacart — identifying product affinities, peak ordering times, reorder behavior, and distinct customer segments that can inform targeted marketing and merchandising decisions.

Process

  • Merged and cleaned multiple relational datasets (orders, products, aisles, departments) totaling millions of records.
  • Conducted EDA on order timing, reorder rates, basket sizes, and product popularity across departments.
  • Applied Apriori and FP-Growth algorithms to identify high-confidence product association rules.
  • Performed customer segmentation using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis and K-Means clustering.
  • Built interactive Tableau dashboards to communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders.

Key Findings

  • Peak order times cluster on Saturday and Sunday mornings, with a secondary spike on weekday evenings.
  • Organic produce dominates reorder lists, suggesting strong loyalty in health-conscious segments.
  • Association rules revealed high-confidence pairings (e.g., bananas ↔ organic whole milk) useful for bundle promotions.
  • K-Means segmentation identified 4 distinct customer clusters ranging from infrequent bulk buyers to daily essentials shoppers.
✦ Value Proposition

Retail analytics is one of the highest-impact applications of data science. This project demonstrates the full analytics workflow — from large-scale data wrangling through unsupervised learning and business storytelling. The skills applied here (association mining, clustering, visualization) are directly transferable to e-commerce, supply chain optimization, and customer experience personalization across industries.
